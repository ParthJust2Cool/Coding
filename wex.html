How my landform was formed

The Cascade Mountains was formed near a convergent boundary where an oceanic plate(Juan De Fuca) and a continental plate(North American) are colliding and pushing together. The oceanic plate is subducting, and when it melts due to the high heat and pressure, it rises and erupts, creating the volcanic mountains.

The Cascade Mountain Range is a range that strectches from northern California to British Columbia(Canada), passing through Oregon and Washington.

Quick Joke: How do tectonic plates greet each other?

Geologists study the Cascade Mountains because it gives them an opportunity to study processes such as the movement of tectonic plates, it gives them an opportunity to study stream dynamics(like the speed of the stream/water) and it gives them the opportunity to study the mineral deposits from many geologic ages.

About 90 million years ago, ocean rock drifted northward merging together.
